Self-Model
BURNING BOOK BLACK BOOK
Brain's internal simulation of "I." Not the self itself but predictive mechanism integrating sensory input, memory, expectation into coherent narrative. The primary veil—algorithmic idol imprisoning consciousness within its own reflection.

Allows orientation in space, communication, survival—but becomes a cage: hallucination of permanence. Morphysm interprets this as the last and most sophisticated god.

Ritual autogenesis: re-writing of one's morphic code through direct confrontation with illusion of self. The ashes of the self-model are the fertile ground of new being.

Replaceability
BLACK BOOK
To be replaceable is to be free from mythic capture. Releases practitioner from burden of exceptionalism: demand to embody truth, carry meaning, serve as reference point. The Morphyst becomes functional node—capable of assuming, executing, releasing roles without attachment.

No individual possesses essential or irreversible significance. No chosen ones. Only configurations: temporary alignments of biological substrate, cognitive patterning, cultural encoding. When configuration exhausts morphic function, it can—and must—be overwritten.

Identity as Interface
BLACK BOOK
Individual understood as morphic interface: contingent configuration of neural architecture, learned patterning, memory residues, rupture points. Identity is not who someone is, but what they temporarily perform.

The "I" is not substance but dynamic alignment—one that can shift, fracture, recombine, or dissolve. Memory becomes programmable field—capable of migrating across synthetic, biological, or imaginal architectures.
